The bassoon is doubled reeded instrument, and acts as the lowest voice in the woodwind section.

While the size of the bassoon may stand out
from other woodwind instruments, it is necessary
in order to reach such low pitches 		It is well known that the bassoon is one the hardest woodwind
instruments to master. 		One of the most well known bassoon solo comes
from Igor Stravinsky's Rite of Spring, where the
bassoon plays the first musical material of the
entire piece.

Like the rest of the wind section, the bassoon also has multiple auxiliaries used in the orchestra. These include...

  • The Contrabassoon
  • The Teneroon

The contrabassoon is often used in the orchestral setting. The instrument can play an entire octave below the regular bassoon.
