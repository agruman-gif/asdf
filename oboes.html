The oboe is a double reeded instrument, which acts as the second highest voice in the woodwind section. ----
The modern oboe. Oboe player John Mack.

The oboe is an important addition to the woodwind section. At the beginning of each orchestral performance, the oboe gives the initial A, which tunes each section of the orchestra.



While the amount of auxiliaries used in the oboe section may not be as expansive as the auxiliaries used in the flute section, they still play a very important role.
Many lyrical lines are written for the oboe and its auxiliaries, which stand out of the orchestra.

The auxiliaries of the oboe include:
  • The english horn
  • The bass oboe
